# Go-To-Market Strategy — AI Spend Audit

## Positioning

**Category:** AI cost optimization for startups  
**Positioning statement:** AI Spend Audit is the free, no-login tool that shows startup teams exactly how much they're overpaying for AI tools and exactly how to fix it.

**Why free + no-login:** Every friction point before value is revenue lost. Requiring signup before showing savings means 60–80% of visitors leave before they see why they should care. Free with instant results means the value proposition is experienced, not promised.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Technical founders and CTOs at seed/Series A startups
- 2–20 person teams
- Paying for 3–8 AI tools
- Spending $100–$800/mo on AI
- Probably not tracking it closely

### Secondary: Engineering managers at growth-stage companies
- 20–100 person teams
- AI spend becoming a budget line item
- Preparing for finance reviews

### Tertiary: Solo founders / indie hackers
- On multiple tools "just in case"
- Budget-conscious

## Launch Channels

### 1. Product Hunt Launch (Day 1)
**Target:** Top 5 of the day

**Preparation:**
- Build "hunter" relationships 2 weeks before
- Prepare 50+ genuine supporters to upvote and comment opening hour
- Write a maker comment that tells the personal origin story ("I found I was paying $400/yr for Gemini Ultra I never used")
- GIF demo that shows the audit running in 30 seconds
- Launch on Tuesday or Wednesday (highest traffic days)

**Key messages:**
- "We found X savings in our own stack building this"
- Show the actual dollar amounts in the listing description
- "No signup required" as a key feature — unusual for SaaS, stands out

### 3. Hacker News

**Post:** "Show HN: AI Spend Audit – find how much you're overpaying for AI tools (free, no login)"

**Key HN-specific elements:**
- Technical credibility: mention the audit engine, open to showing rules
- Acknowledge limitations: "currently covers 8 tools, adding more based on demand"
- Be specific about savings: share a real example from your own audit
- Be responsive in comments for first 2 hours

**Expected outcome:** 50–200 upvotes, 20–50 comments, 500–3,000 visitors

## Growth Loop

```
User runs audit
    ↓
Shareable report URL generated
    ↓
User shares on Twitter/Slack ("I found $X/mo I was wasting")
    ↓
Followers click → run their own audit
    ↓
Viral coefficient > 1 = organic growth
```

The share report is the most important growth mechanism. Every shareable link carries the savings number in the URL preview. This is free advertising that aligns perfectly with our value proposition.
